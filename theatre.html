Shakespeare theatre
The Shakespeare Theatre is a very impressive architectural and historical building. Today, it is stands as one of Gdansk’s major sites to see. What we can see today is a rebuilt version built in 1990 by Jerzy Limon.
The original one at first it was not only used as a theatre but also as a fencing school as well as a place for animal fights. It was located at the edges of the city as the church sometimes did not approve of the existence of the building. People often came here as the cheapest tickets were around the price of a beer.
The building was made from wood and had an open roof. It symmetrical on all sides, making it more visible for the audience, but also made it hard to use props as all of them would have to be used on all sides. Actors all around the world came here to perform here and the shows were frequently a mix of languages.
After it has been destroyed, it has been rebuilt and even now stands with all its glory, with multiple performing stages and people watching from all around the world. Today, as a symbol of its past it has a roof that can open at one of the stages, sometimes opened during a major performance.
